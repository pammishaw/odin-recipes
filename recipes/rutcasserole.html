Rutabaga Casserole

Description

Rutabaga casserole, a hearty and comforting dish, is a traditional favorite in Nordic cuisine, especially during the winter holidays. Made primarily with mashed rutabagas—also known as swedes—the casserole is often combined with cream, eggs, and a touch of sweetness from brown sugar or syrup. Some versions include breadcrumbs or a sprinkle of nutmeg for added flavor and texture. The dish is then baked until golden and bubbling, resulting in a creamy interior and a lightly crisp top. Its mellow, earthy taste pairs well with roasted meats and other savory holiday sides.

Beyond its cultural roots, rutabaga casserole is also a nutritious choice. Rutabagas are a good source of fiber, vitamin C, and potassium, and they have a lower carbohydrate content than potatoes, making them a great option for people watching their carb intake. The casserole can be easily adapted to suit different diets, such as using dairy-free cream alternatives or incorporating protein-rich toppings like crushed nuts or seeds. Whether served at a festive table or as a cozy side dish on a chilly evening, rutabaga casserole offers a unique blend of simplicity, tradition, and nourishment.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs rutabagas, washed, peeled, and diced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp syrup
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 3 tbsp butter, chilled

    • Instructions

    1. Cook the diced rutabagas by boiling in a pot of water for about 35-40 minutes, until tender. Drain and empty into a large bowl. Mash with a potato masher.
    2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. While it is heating, add the heavy cream, syrup, egg, and spices to the rutabaga mash. Mix well.
    3. Grease a casserole dish. Pour the mash mixture into the dish and cover with the breadcrumbs. Place pats of the butter evenly along the top.
    4. Bake for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the top is golden brown. Allow to cool and enjoy as a side to your favorite entree.
