Norwegian Brown Cheese Meatballs

Description

Root vegetables, hearty grains, and dairy products form the backbone of many traditional dishes—none more beloved than the meatball, or kjøttkaker. While meatballs are common throughout the Nordic region, Norway has its own distinctive twist: a rich, savory gravy made with brunost, or brown cheese. This sweet and tangy cheese, made from caramelized whey, adds a uniquely Nordic depth of flavor that sets these meatballs apart.

Norwegian brown cheese meatballs are typically made with a blend of ground beef or pork, though modern variations may use poultry or plant-based alternatives. The meatballs are seasoned simply with salt, pepper, and sometimes nutmeg, then pan-fried and simmered in a silky gravy infused with brunost. The result is a beautifully balanced dish—hearty and savory with a subtle hint of sweetness. Traditionally served with boiled potatoes, lingonberry jam, and steamed vegetables or cabbage, these meatballs are a staple of Norwegian family dinners, holidays, and Sunday meals. They represent the kind of humble, flavorful cooking that makes Scandinavian food so satisfying.

Ingredients

For the meatballs:

  • 1 1/4 lb ground meat of choice
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp allspice
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 onion, finely diced
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 1/2 ounces Norwegian brown goat cheese, grated
  • 2 tbsp potato starch

For the gravy:

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups stock of choice
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients for the meatballs into one large bowl. Form about 20 meatballs from the mixture.
  2. Heat a large sautee pan over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of oil and cook the meatballs, turning to brown on all sides, for about 5 minutes. Set this pan aside.
  3. Place a large pan over medium heat. Melt the butter and wisk in the flour. Cook with frequent stirring for about 6 to 8 minutes until browned.
  4. Gradually add the stock to the pan while continuing to stir then throw in the salt.
  5. Pour over the meatballs in the other pan and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and allow to simmer for about 15 minutes, until the meatballs are cooked through. Adjust the seasonings as needed.
  6. Serve with potatoes, lingonberry jam, or your favorite roasted vegetables.
