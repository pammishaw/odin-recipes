Lohikeitto (Creamy Salmon Soup)

Description

The cool, pristine waters of the Nordic region are home to an abundance of fish, with salmon being one of the most prized catches. For centuries, this rich natural resource has played a central role in shaping the local cuisine, inspiring dishes that are both nourishing and deeply rooted in tradition.

Among these is lohikeitto (the Finnish term for "salmon soup"), a traditional Finnish dish known for its creamy, comforting flavors and simple, wholesome ingredients. This beloved Nordic dish features tender chunks of fresh salmon simmered in a broth with potatoes, leeks, and carrots, often enriched with cream or milk for a silky texture. It's typically seasoned with dill, a signature herb in Finnish cuisine, which adds a fresh, aromatic note. Lohikeitto is hearty yet delicate, making it a perfect dish for cold weather or cozy, home-cooked meals. Often served with dark rye bread on the side, it’s a staple of Finnish home cooking that beautifully showcases the region’s love for clean, natural flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 lb salmon fillet, cut to bite-sized chunks
  • 2 leeks, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 3 medium potatoes, diced
  • 6 cups fish or vegetable stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 0.5 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Place a pot over medium heat. Melt the unsalted butter in the pot and sautee the sliced leeks until translucent (about 5-7 minutes).
  2. Add the carrots, potato, and stock. Bring the mixture to a boil and let cook until the vegetables become tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  3. Add the salmon as well as salt and pepper. Allow to boil for another 10 minutes, until the salmon has cooked through.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and toss in the dill. Boil for 5 more minutes.
  5. Adjust the seasonings to your liking. Ladle into bowls, serve with your favorite bread, and enjoy!
