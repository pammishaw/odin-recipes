Swedish Apple Cake

Description

Swedish apple cake, or äppelkaka, is a beloved dessert that captures the essence of Nordic home baking—simple, comforting, and full of seasonal flavor. Typically made with a buttery vanilla-scented batter, the cake is layered or topped with thinly sliced apples dusted in cinnamon and sugar. As it bakes, the apples soften and sink slightly into the golden crust, creating a tender, fragrant treat that’s often served warm. Unlike heavily frosted or overly sweet desserts, äppelkaka strikes a delicate balance between sweetness and the natural tartness of the apples, making it a favorite for afternoon fika or a cozy family dinner.

What makes Swedish apple cake especially cherished is its versatility and tradition. Some versions include breadcrumbs or almond flour for extra texture, while others are topped with a sprinkle of pearl sugar or slivered almonds. It’s most delicious when made with tart, firm apples—often picked fresh in early autumn. While delicious on its own, it’s often served with a spoonful of vanilla custard, whipped cream, or a drizzle of warm vanilla sauce. More than just a dessert, äppelkaka is a taste of Swedish hospitality and the comforting rhythm of the seasons.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3 cups apple, peeled and diced
  • 1/3 cup walnuts, chopped

Instructions

  1. Mix together the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg in a large bowl until uniformly combined.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the butter and sugar until all the sugar is incorporated. Add the eggs, one at a time, aling with the and whisk until well-combined.
  3. Fold the liquid ingredients into the dry ingredients until well-combined. Add in the apples and nuts. Fold until relatively uniform.
  4. Grease a 9-inch baking dish and heat the oven to 350 degrees F. Pour the batter into the dish and bake for about 40 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean after pressing into the cake.
  5. Allow the cake to cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
