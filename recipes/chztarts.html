Nordic Cheese Tart

Description

The Nordic cheese tart is a savory-sweet pastry that captures the essence of Scandinavian comfort with a touch of elegance. Built on a buttery shortcrust base, the tart is filled with a rich, creamy blend of soft Nordic cheeses—like Nøkkelost or a mild goat cheese—lightly whipped with eggs and a hint of crème fraîche. Baked until golden and just set, the filling is velvety and slightly tangy, balanced by the flaky, crisp crust. Some versions incorporate caramelized onions, dill, or a whisper of nutmeg, adding depth and warmth to the delicate cheese flavor. It’s a dish that feels both humble and refined—perfect for a cozy fika or a festive smørgåsbord.

Traditionally enjoyed at room temperature, the Nordic cheese tart pairs beautifully with crisp greens or a side of lingonberry preserves for contrast. It reflects the Nordic philosophy of simple ingredients treated with care, where quality and seasonality matter more than flash. Whether served in small wedges as part of a larger meal or as a standalone lunch with a cup of hot tea, it offers a quiet satisfaction—the kind that lingers long after the last bite. This tart is a testament to how even the most modest ingredients, when brought together thoughtfully, can become something truly memorable.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 egg
  • 1-2 tsp cold water

For the filling:

  • 4 eggs
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup gouda, grated
  • 1 cup aged cheddar, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Combine the flour and salt in a large bowl. Add the butter and toss to coat. Mix by hand or food processor until the mixture has a crumbly, sand-like consistency.
  2. Add the egg and mix by hand or food processor until the dough holds together when pressed between your fingers.
  3. Form the dough into a ball, wrap in plastic wrap, and allow it to sit in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes. In the meantime, heat your oven to 390 degrees F.
  4. Oil a tart pan and roll out the dough until large enough to fill the pan. Carefully lay the dough into the pan and trim off any excess.
  5. Combine the filling ingredients into a large bowl. Pour the batter into the tart pan and bake for about 45 minutes, until the tart becomes a golden brown.
  6. Serve while still warm with lingonberry jam or your favorite salad.
